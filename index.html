Bio

I am currently a third-year industrial (CIFRE) Ph.D. student in deep learning at TDK InvenSense France and the INRIA Grenoble Rhône-Alpes in the Statify research team.

My research focuses on bringing the power of neural networks to very constrained micro-embedded systems #TinyML. My interests include general deep learning, model compression, pruning, knowledge distilliation, quantization, tinyML, edge inference… My supervisors are Etienne de Foras, Sr. software engineer at InvenSense and Julyan Arbel, researcher at the INRIA.

In 2019, I graduated as an engineer in Computer Science & Engineering specialized in Data Mining & Decision Support at the Université de Technologie de Compiègne, France (UTC). In parallel of the last year of engineering course, I also obtained the Double European Master degree EMECIS in Machine Learning & Optimization between the UTC and the Scuola Politecnica di Genova, Italy, to specialize in research. For my Master thesis, I worked as a research engineer intern in Deep Learning at InvenSense to get familiar with my Ph.D. subject.

I am passionate about innovation, research, artificial intelligence, as well as software development in general. I have a deep interest to collaborate within rich multi-cultural settings.

Preliminary research study for a CIFRE Ph.D. thesis supervised by Thibaut Arribe, Ph.D. @Kelis and Stéphane Crozat, research professor @Université de Technologie de Compiègne.
The goal was to propose and research what states (e.g. test average, variance, behaviors, hesitations…) could be recover from a large database of singular events, to design a learning dashboard for professors to help identifying first-year students difficulties in STEM for Faq2sciences website in a Scenari context. Objectives included:

  • Analyzed data (test results, answering time) using ElasticSearch to understand student progress and behavior (≈75.000 quiz samples)
  • Designed a learning dashboard from the analysis with Kibana and then Plotly, intended for an integration to Faq2sciences website.

Keywords: Research, Aggregation, Event-State recovery, Data analysis, Data visualization, Learning Dashboards, NoSQL
